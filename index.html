Israeli Drone Strikes on Basij & Internal Security Forces: Starting ~Mar 11, Israel deployed AI-guided drone swarms against Basij militia checkpoints in Tehran and other cities, targeting internal security forces responsible for protest suppression. The IDF confirmed targeting Basij members using a “mother launcher” platform deploying multiple AI-equipped drones. WSJ reported Iranian civilians transmitted Basij checkpoint locations to Israel via social media. This represents a strategic shift from military infrastructure to internal security apparatus — directly undermining regime coercive capacity and significantly strengthening Scenario C (Regime Fracture) probability. Interception rate Phase III: ~85–90% overall (Israel + Gulf combined); however, Iran’s sheer volume produced significant leakage — at least 19 commercial ships, multiple residential buildings, and military facilities hit.
Day 18 Developments — Kharg Island & Gulf Escalation: CENTCOM struck 90+ military targets on Kharg Island (Iran’s main oil export hub, ~90% of export crude), destroying naval mine storage, missile bunkers, and military facilities. Oil infrastructure was deliberately preserved. Trump: “destroyed 100% of Iran’s Military capability” at Kharg, warned oil would be next. Iran retaliated with unprecedented Gulf-wide barrage: UAE (9 BMs + 33 drones — Fujairah port fire), Saudi Arabia (6 BMs intercepted al-Kharj; 26 drones downed), Qatar (4 BMs + drones intercepted), Kuwait (airport damaged, Al-Jaber AB radar hit). This represents Iran’s strategic pivot from declining Israel-directed BMs to Gulf-state targeting — a significant escalation. Trump called for warship coalition (US, UK, France, Japan, S. Korea, China) to reopen Hormuz.
